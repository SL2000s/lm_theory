Theorem 1: Jacobian Lipschitz Norm Theorem

Statement

Let $f: \mathbb{R}^n \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^m$ be differentiable and Lipschitz continuous under a choice of $p$-norm $\|\cdot\|_p$. Let $J_f(x)$ denote its total derivative (Jacobian) at $x$. Then $\lip_p(f) = \sup_{\mathbf{x}\in \mathbb{R}^n} \|J_f(\mathbf{x})\|_p$ where $\|J_f(\mathbf{x})\|_p$ is the induced operator norm on $J_f(\mathbf{x})$.
