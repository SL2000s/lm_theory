Definition 4: Sparse Attention

Statement

Sparse attention: the low-temperature limit $a_t \approx \delta_{tt^*}$ and $\Delta x = m_{t^*}$, where $\delta$ is the Kronecker delta. This occurs when there is a large difference between the top two scores: $t^* = \mathrm{argmax}_t w_t$ and $w_{t^*} - \max_{t\neq t^*} w_t \gg 1$.

Motivation of Statement (AI-Generated)

Sparse attention is a powerful concept in machine learning, particularly in the context of neural networks and transformers. It allows the model to focus on the most relevant parts of the input data, significantly reducing computational complexity and improving efficiency. This is especially useful when dealing with large datasets or sequences, where full attention mechanisms would be computationally prohibitive. By leveraging sparse attention, models can achieve faster training times and better scalability without sacrificing performance.
