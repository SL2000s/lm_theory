Definition 6: Circuit Collapse

Statement

Circuit collapse: spontaneous phase transition in which a sparse attention distribution selects a different token due to noise on $\{q, k_t\}$. Let $\epsilon^w_t = k_t^T\epsilon^q + q^T\epsilon^k_t + \mathcal{O}({\epsilon^q}^T\epsilon^k_t)$ be perturbations on $w_t$ that result from $\epsilon^q$ and $\epsilon^k_t$. Circuit collapse occurs when there exists a $t \neq t^*$ for which $w_{t^*} - w_t < \epsilon^w_t - \epsilon^w_{t^*}$.

Motivation of Statement (AI-Generated)

Understanding the concept of circuit collapse is crucial in the study of attention mechanisms within neural networks. It describes how small perturbations in the query and key vectors can lead to significant changes in the attention distribution, potentially causing the model to focus on different tokens. This phenomenon is particularly important when analyzing the robustness and stability of attention-based models, such as transformers, in the presence of noise. By recognizing and mitigating circuit collapse, one can improve the reliability and performance of these models in various applications, including natural language processing and machine translation.
