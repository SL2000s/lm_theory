Lemma 7: Matrix Norm Bound Lemma

Statement

$\|Y^\top P^{(i)}Y\|_2 \leq \phi^{-1}(N-1)$

Motivation of Statement (AI-Generated)

The given inequality $\|Y^\top P^{(i)}Y\|_2 \leq \phi^{-1}(N-1)$ is useful in the context of matrix analysis and optimization. It provides a bound on the spectral norm of a transformed matrix, which can be critical when assessing the stability and performance of numerical algorithms. This statement is particularly valuable when dealing with large-scale data or complex systems where ensuring that certain matrix properties are within specific limits is essential for accurate and efficient computations.

Original Proof

$\|Y^\top P^{(i)}Y\|_2=\|\mathrm{Cov}(\mathbb{Y})\|_2 = \lambda_{\max}(\mathrm{Cov}(\mathbb{Y})) \leq \Tr(\mathrm{Cov}(\mathbb{Y})) \leq \phi^{-1}(N-1)$, where the first equality holds by symmetry of $\mathrm{Cov}(\mathbb{Y})$ and the next holds by $\mathrm{Cov}(\mathbb{Y})$ being positive semi-definite, so all its eigenvalues are non-negative, and hence the maximal eigenvalue is bounded by the sum of the eigenvalues, equal to its trace. The final inequality is from Lemma Lemma 3.
