Lemma 6: Block row norm inequality

Statement

Let $A$ be a block matrix with block rows $A_1, \ldots, A_N$. Then $\|A\|_2 \leq \sqrt{\sum_i \|A_i\|_2^2}$, and equality holds if and only if the first right singular vectors of the $A_i$ align.

Motivation of Statement (AI-Generated)

The given inequality for block matrices is particularly useful in numerical linear algebra and matrix analysis. It provides an upper bound for the 2-norm of a block matrix in terms of the 2-norms of its block rows. This can be applied in scenarios where the matrix is too large to handle directly, but its blocks are more manageable. Additionally, the condition for equality helps in understanding the structure of the matrix and the alignment of its singular vectors, which can be crucial for optimization problems and in the study of matrix decompositions.

Original Proof

\begin{equation*} \|A\|_2^2 = \left\Vert \begin{bmatrix} A_1 \\ \vdots \\ A_N \\ \end{bmatrix}\right\Vert_2^2 = \sup_{\|\mathbf{x}\|_2=1} \left\Vert\begin{bmatrix} A_1 \\ \vdots \\ A_N \\ \end{bmatrix} \mathbf{x}\right\Vert_2^2 = \sup_{\|\mathbf{x}\|_2=1} \sum_i \|A_i \mathbf{x}\|_2^2 \leq \sum_i \sup_{\|\mathbf{x}\|_2=1} \|A_i \mathbf{x}\|_2^2 = \sum_i \|A_i\|_2^2. \end{equation*} Note that equality holds if and only if the first right singular vectors of the $A_i$ align.

Explanation of Proof (AI-Generated)

To understand the proof, let's break it down into several steps:

1. Norm Definition: The proof starts by defining the squared 2-norm of matrix \(A\) as the squared 2-norm of a block matrix composed of submatrices \(A_i\):
\[ \|A\|_2^2 = \left\Vert \begin{bmatrix} A_1 \\ \vdots \\ A_N \\ \end{bmatrix}\right\Vert_2^2 \]

2. Supremum Representation: The squared 2-norm is then expressed as the supremum over all unit vectors \(\mathbf{x}\):
\[ \sup_{\|\mathbf{x}\|_2=1} \left\Vert\begin{bmatrix} A_1 \\ \vdots \\ A_N \\ \end{bmatrix} \mathbf{x}\right\Vert_2^2 \]

3. Sum of Norms: This supremum is expanded into a sum of squared norms of the submatrices \(A_i\) applied to \(\mathbf{x}\):
\[ \sup_{\|\mathbf{x}\|_2=1} \sum_i \|A_i \mathbf{x}\|_2^2 \]

4. Supremum of Each Term: The sum of supremums is then bounded by the sum of the supremums of each individual term:
\[ \leq \sum_i \sup_{\|\mathbf{x}\|_2=1} \|A_i \mathbf{x}\|_2^2 \]

5. Norm of Submatrices: Finally, each supremum is recognized as the squared 2-norm of the submatrix \(A_i\):
\[ = \sum_i \|A_i\|_2^2 \]

Thus, the proof shows that:
\[ \|A\|_2^2 \leq \sum_i \|A_i\|_2^2 \]

Note that equality holds if and only if the first right singular vectors of the \(A_i\) align.
