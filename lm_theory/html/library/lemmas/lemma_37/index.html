Lemma 37: Gradient Inequality for Optimal Tokens

Statement

Suppose Assumption $\ref{assum:loss:prope}$ holds and let $\op=(\op_i)_{i=1}^n$ be the unique optimal tokens with $ \Wm$ denoting the SVM solution. Fix any $\mu>0$ (per Lemma Lemma 36). For any choice of $\pi>0$, there exists $R:=R_{\pi,\mu}$ such that, for any $ \W\in \conb_{\mu,R}(\Wm)$ we have \[ \li \nabla\Lc(\W), \frac{\W}{\tf{\W}} \ri\geq (1+\pi)\li \nabla\Lc(\W), \frac{\Wm}{\tf{\Wm}}\ri. \]

Motivation of Statement (AI-Generated)

This result is particularly useful in the context of Support Vector Machines (SVM) optimization. It provides a bound on the gradient of the loss function, ensuring that the optimal solution $\Wm$ is robust within a certain neighborhood. This can be applied to guarantee the stability and performance of the SVM model under small perturbations, making it a valuable tool for both theoretical analysis and practical implementations of SVMs.

Original Proof

We provide the proof in four steps:
Step~1: There are no stationary points within $\conb_{\mu,R_\mu}(\Wm)$ for sufficiently large $R_\mu$. This step directly follows from Lemma Lemma 36: for all $\V,\W\in \Sc_{\mu}(\Wm)$ with $\tf{\V}=\tf{\Wm}$ and $\tf{\W}\geq R_\mu$, it follows from Lemma Lemma 36 that there exists $R_\mu$ such that $\iprod{\V}{ -\nabla \Lc(\W)}$ is strictly positive.
Step~2: Consider an arbitrary value of $\epsilon \in (0, \mu/2)$ and let $1/(1+\pi)=1-\epsilon$. It follows from Lemma Lemma 37 that, there exists $R_\epsilon$ such that all $\W \in \conb_{\mu,R_\epsilon}(\Wm)$ satisfy \begin{align}\label{eqn:neg:corr:local:nabla0} \iprod{-\nabla\mc{L}(\W)}{\frac{\Wm}{\tf{\Wm}}} \geq (1-\epsilon) \iprod{-\nabla \mc{L}(\W)}{\frac{\W}{\tf{\W}}}. \end{align} Step~3: Updates remain inside the cone $\conb_{\mu,R_\mu}(\Wm)$. By leveraging the results from Step 1 and Step 2, we demonstrate that the gradient iterates remain within this cone. Note that by our assumption and for sufficiently large $\eta_0$, we get $\W(1) \in \conb_{\mu,R}(\Wm)$. Here, $R$ is chosen such that \begin{align}\label{eqn:bound:R:nabla0} R \geq R_\mu \vee R_\epsilon \vee 1/2. \end{align} We provide the proof by induction. By the above argument, $\W(1) \in \conb_{\mu,R}(\Wm)$. Let $\W(k) \in \conb_{\mu,R}(\Wm)$. For all $k\geq 1$, we have that \begin{subequations}\label{eqn:rho:def:nabla0} \begin{align} & \min_{t\neq \op_i,~i\in[n]} ~~ \left\langle (\x_{i\op_i}-\x_\itt) \z_i^\top, - \nabla \mc{L}(\W(k)) \right\rangle \geq \bar{\rho} , \label{eqn:bar:rho} \\ &\frac{1}{1-\epsilon} \iprod{ {\frac{\Wm}{\tf{\Wm}}}}{-\nabla\mc{L}(\W(k))} \geq \rho. \end{align} \end{subequations} for some positive constants $\bar{\rho}$ and $\rho$. To proceed, for all $t\neq \op_i,~i\in[n]$, we obtain \begin{equation}\label{eqn:localgd:1:nabla0} \begin{split} \left\langle (\x_{i\op_i}-\x_\itt) \z_i^\top, \frac{\W(k+1)}{\tf{\W(k)}} \right\rangle &= \left\langle (\x_{i\op_i}-\x_\itt) \z_i^\top, \frac{\W(k)}{\tf{\W(k)}} -\frac{\eta}{\tf{\W(k)}}\nabla \mc{L}(\W(k)) \right\rangle\\ &\ge \mu \Theta- \frac{\eta}{\tf{\W(k)}} \left\langle (\x_{i\op_i}-\x_\itt) \z_i^\top, \nabla \mc{L}(\W(k)) \right\rangle \\ & \geq \mu \Theta +\frac{\eta\bar{\rho}}{\tf{\W(k)}}. \end{split} \end{equation} Here, the first inequality follows from the induction assumption $\W(k) \in \conb_{\mu,R}(\Wm)$, and the second inequality uses \eqref{eqn:bar:rho}. From Lemma Lemma 36, we have $\left\langle \nabla \Lc(\W(k)),\W(k)\right\rangle<0$ which implies that $\tf{\W(k+1)} \geq \tf{\W(k)}$. Hence, \begin{align*} \tf{\W(k+1)}&\leq\frac{1}{{2\tf{\W(k)}}} \left(\tf{\W(k+1)}^2+\tf{\W(k)}^2\right)\\ & = \frac{1}{2\tf{\W(k)}} \left(2\tf{W(t)}^2-2\eta\left\langle \nabla \Lc(\W(k)),\W(k)\right\rangle+\eta^2\tf{\nabla \Lc(\W(k))}^2\right)\\ &\leq \tf{\W(k)}- \frac{\eta}{\tf{\W(k)}}\left\langle \nabla \Lc(\W(k)),\W(k)\right\rangle + \eta^2 \|\nabla \Lc(\W(k))\|^2, \end{align*} where the last inequality follows from $\tf{\W(k)} \geq R \geq 1/2 $. Thus, \begin{equation}\label{eqn:localgd:2:nabla0} \begin{split} \frac{\tf{\W(k+1)}}{\tf{\W(k)}}& \leq 1- \eta \left\langle \nabla \Lc(\W(k)),\frac{\W(k)}{\tf{\W(k)}} \right\rangle + \eta^2 \frac{\|\nabla \mc{L}(\W(k))\|^2}{\tf{\W(k)}}\\ & \leq 1- \frac{\eta}{1-\epsilon} \iprod{\nabla\mc{L}(\W(k))} {\frac{\Wm}{\tf{\Wm}}}+ \eta^2 \frac{\|\nabla \mc{L}(\W(k))\|^2}{\tf{\W(k)}}\\ & \leq 1 + \frac{\eta \rho}{\tf{\W(k)}} + \frac{\eta^2\|\nabla \mc{L}(\W(k))\|^2}{\tf{\W(k)}}=C_1(\rho,\eta). \end{split} \end{equation} Now, it follows from \eqref{eqn:localgd:1:nabla0} and \eqref{eqn:localgd:2:nabla0} that \begin{equation}\label{eqn:localgd:3:nabla0} \begin{split} \min_{t\neq \op_i,~i\in[n]} ~~ \left\langle (\x_{i\op_i}-\x_\itt) \z_i^\top, \frac{\W(k+1)}{\tf{\W(k+1)}}\right\rangle &\geq \frac{1}{C_1(\bar{\rho},\eta)} \left(\mu \Theta+\frac{\eta \bar{\rho}}{\tf{\W(k)}}\right)\\ & \geq \mu \Theta+\frac{\eta}{C_1(\bar{\rho},\eta)} \left(\frac{\bar{\rho}-\rho\mu \Theta}{\tf{\W(k)}} - \eta \mu \Theta \frac{\|\nabla \mc{L}(\W(k))\|^2 }{\tf{\W(k)}}\right)\\ & \geq \mu \Theta. \end{split} \end{equation} Here, we leverage the fact that the parameter $\mu$ can be chosen arbitrarily, and then we select the step size $\eta$ to satisfy the following condition: $$\eta \leq \frac{\bar{\rho}-\rho\mu \Theta }{\mu\Theta} \frac{1} {\|\nabla \mc{L}(\W(k))\|^2}. $$ Hence, \eqref{eqn:localgd:3:nabla0} gives $\W(k+1) \in \conb_{\mu,R}$.
Step 4: The correlation of $\W(k)$ and $\Wm$ increases over $k$. It follows from Theorem Theorem 22 that $\left\Vert \W\left(k\right)\right\Vert \rightarrow\infty$. Hence, we can choose $k_0$ such that for any $k\ge k_0$, it holds that $\tf{\W(k)}> R$ for some $R \geq R_\mu \vee R_\epsilon \vee 1/2$. Now, following similar steps in \eqref{eqn:decpath:1} and \eqref{eqn:decpath:22}, for some constant $C(\epsilon,\eta)$, we obtain \begin{align*} \left\langle\frac{\W(k)}{\tf{\W(k)}}, \frac{\Wm}{\tf{\Wm}} \right\rangle \ge1-\epsilon+ \frac{C(\epsilon,\eta)}{\tf{\W(k)}}. \end{align*} Consequently, \begin{align*} \liminf_{t\to\infty}\iprod{\frac{\W(k)}{\tf{\W(k)}}}{\frac{\Wm}{\tf{\Wm}}}\ge1-\epsilon. \end{align*} Since $\epsilon \in (0, \mu/2)$ is arbitrary, we get $\W(k)/\tf{\W(k)}\to \Wm/\tf{\Wm}$.
